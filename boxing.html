Boxing

"The Sweet Science"

Boxing, often called “The Sweet Science” by its most ardent followers, is a combat sport that harkens back to the day of Ancient Greece. A one-on-one contest that pits two participants of the same weight class against one another, boxing is a sport that represents determination, physical and mental determination, national pride, and courage. Throughout its storied past, boxers have come from all nations, yet boxers from Eastern Europe, the UK, Cuba, Mexico, and the US dominate today’s current scene.

Hailing from Kazakhstan, middleweight champion Gennady Gennadyevich Golovkin, aka “GGG” or “Triple G”, is one of the most feared boxers on the planet. He combines a high-pressure style with one of the best jabs in all of boxing and a devastating right-hand, that has led to 33 knockouts in 37 fights.

Ukrainian-born Vasyl “Hi-Tech” Lomachenko is probably the most skilled boxers. With varied training in martial arts as well as ballet as a child, Lomachenko has the dexterity and movement that almost all other boxers lack. Not the biggest puncher, he instead overwhelms his opponents with a combination of speed, elite technique, and masterful footwork. He is a two-time Gold Medalist.

Anthony Joshua is a monster of a man. At 6’6” and 250 lbs of nearly all muscle, he is one of the most physically imposing boxers. This muscle translates into impressive power, as AJ sports a 100% knockout rate. Though his excessive muscle may detract from his stamina and endurance, he often doesn’t need it since his fights rarely last until the late rounds.
